  <h2 itemprop="headline" id="toc_what-is-an-ip-address">What is an IP Address?</h2>
  <p itemprop="text">
   An IP address is a unique identifier assigned to your device on the internet. It plays a crucial role in online communication, allowing websites and services to recognize and interact with your device.

    <h3 id="toc_types-of-ip-addresses">Types of IP Addresses</h3>
    <ul>
      <li><strong><a href="https://www.whatismyip.com/ipv4/">IPv4</a>:</strong> IPv4 is the fourth version of the Internet Protocol, and itâs the most widely used. It assigns a 32-bit address to devices (like 192.168.1.1), allowing for about 4.3 billion unique addresses.</li>
      <li><strong><a href="https://www.whatismyip.com/ipv6/">IPv6</a>:</strong> IPv6 is the newer version of the Internet Protocol, designed to replace IPv4. It uses 128-bit addresses (like <span class="break-ip">2001:0db8:85a3:0000:0000:8a2e:0370:7334</span>), supporting trillions of unique IP addresses to handle the growing number of internet-connected devices.</li>
      <li><strong><a href="https://www.whatismyip.com/public-vs-private-ip-address/#h-what-is-a-public-ip-address">Public IP</a>:</strong> The address assigned to your network by your Internet Service Provider (ISP).</li>
      <li><strong><a href="https://www.whatismyip.com/public-vs-private-ip-address/#h-what-is-a-private-ip-address">Private IP</a>:</strong> The internal address used within your home or office network.</li>
      <li><strong><a href="https://www.whatismyip.com/static-ip-vs-dynamic-ip/#h-what-is-a-static-ip-address">Static IP</a>:</strong> An IP address that doesnât change over time.</li>
      <li><strong><a href="https://www.whatismyip.com/static-ip-vs-dynamic-ip/#h-what-is-a-dynamic-ip-address">Dynamic IP</a>:</strong> An IP address that changes periodically, typically assigned by ISPs.</li>
